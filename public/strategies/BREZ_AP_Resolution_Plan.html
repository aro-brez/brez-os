The offer structure

Option A • Default

Promissory note

  • Interest Rate: 10% annually
  • First 6 Months: Interest accrues, no cash payments
  • After 6 Months: Quarterly payments begin
  • Maturity: 2 years
  • Security: Unsecured

This is the default option. No action required to accept.

Option B • Opt-In Required

Equity conversion

  • Premium: 20% bump on amount owed
  • Conversion: Amount converts to equity at next raise
  • Example: $50K owed = $60K equity value
  • Timing: Converts at next qualified financing

Requires explicit opt-in within 7 days.

Hybrid option

Vendors may split their balance between Option A and Option B (e.g., 50% note, 50% equity conversion).

Key principles

From Nick (MP Capital) — Encode these for all vendor negotiations:

01
Underpromise, overdeliver
Set expectations conservatively. Then exceed them. Trust is built through pattern, not promises.
02
First to speak loses
After sending the offer, wait 48 hours minimum. Let them process. Don't fill silence with concessions.
03
Pattern beats promise
What we DO matters more than what we SAY. Consistent execution builds credibility.
04
Execute regardless of response
Don't let vendor reactions dictate business operations. Stay the course.
05
New money rules
Don't hamstring future fundraising with onerous terms. Preserve optionality.
