1. Executive Summary: Three Core Decisions

Decision A: BREZ Core Mark

Action: HOLD - Wait for Breeze vs. Breez dispute to resolve

Timeline: Monitor quarterly (next check: April 2026)

Strategy: Once resolved, argue BREZ functional beverages are distinct from smokables (Breeze Smoke) and mints/distribution (Breez). Alternative: Register as "BREZ" (B-r-e-z) without macron.

Cost: $0 now (maintain existing application)

Decision B: File Subbrand Trademarks

Action: File NEW applications using compound marks matching actual packaging

Format: "[BRAND] [FLAVOR] FEEL GOOD TONIC"

Examples:

  • FLOW LEMON ELDERFLOWER FEEL GOOD TONIC
  • MUSE [FLAVOR] FEEL GOOD TONIC
  • PASSION [FLAVOR] FEEL GOOD TONIC
  • AMPLIFY [FLAVOR] FEEL GOOD TONIC
  • ELEVATE [FLAVOR] FEEL GOOD TONIC
  • DREAM [FLAVOR] FEEL GOOD TONIC
  • DRIFT [FLAVOR] FEEL GOOD TONIC

Why this works: Matches actual can labeling, creates distinct commercial impression, preserves common law rights to use standalone words (FLOW, MUSE, etc.)

Decision C: Abandon Low-Value Marks

Action: ABANDON by March 1, 2026

Marks: BREZ Juice, BREZ WATER, BREZ WTR, MICRODOSED CANNABIS

Reason: 15-20% success probability does not justify $8,500-$10,000 per mark

Savings: ~$35,000+ avoided spend
